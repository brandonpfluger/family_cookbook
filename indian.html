Curry Chicken

Ingredients

1 onion chopped fine (put in as much as you want for flavor)
2 tbs oil
2-3 tbs curry paste (for the best results, mix mild and extra hot)
2-4 cut up chicken breasts
¼ cup tomato sauce or 2 whole tomatoes cut up
salt to taste
about ¾ cup water
about ½ tsp sugar
cut up new potatoes

Directions

Chop or grate onion into large pot. Add oil and sauté the onion. Add 2-3 tablespoons of curry paste and brown with the onion. Put in cut up chicken breast and cook until white on both sides (it can be 2-4 breasts, depending on how much meat you want). Stir in tomato sauce or put in 2 whole tomatoes cut up. Salt to taste. Put a dash of water (about ¾ cup). Add some new potatoes if you want. You can put as much curry and tomato product as you want; just start with this and go from there. Cover with a lid and cook about 30-45 minutes. Put over rice.

Dahl

Ingredients

2 cups split peas
3 tbs oil
1 onion, finely chopped
salt and pepper to taste

Directions

Rinse split pease thoroughly. Put peas in a pan and fill it with water. Add 3 tablespoons of oil, the onion, and salt and pepper to taste. Put over rice.

Roti

Ingredients

4 tbs butter Crisco
2 cups flour
⅔ cup water
dash of salt

Directions

In a bowl, mix together the flour and salt. Make a well in the flour and add the Criscro and water. Knead the dough. If the dough is too dry, add a little water; if it is too sticky, add some flour. Once you've kneaded about 5 minutes, cover the bowl with a towel and let sit about 30 minutes. Flour the surface you are going to roll the dough on. Divide the dough to equal portions and roll each piece out semi-thin. On a dry, heated skillet, put one on at a time. Some will puff up; this is o.k. When it puffs up, it is usually time to flip it. You will see brown spots on the dough; this is normal.

Samosa Pastry

Ingredients

2 cups flour
1 tsp salt
¼ cup butter
7 tbs plain yogurt
1 slightly beaten egg white with a little bit of water

Directions

Sift together flour and salt. Melt butter and add to flour. Stir in yogurt. Work with hands until satin-y and smooth. Roll pastry very thin on lightly floured board. Cut into circles with 2½ inch cutter. Brush edges lightly with egg. Place ½ or more teaspoon of filling in center of each. Fold over and, with fork, make sure they are sealed. Fry in deep fat.
