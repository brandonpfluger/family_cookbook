Angel Mallow Frosting

Ingredients

½ cup sugar
2 egg whites
2 tbs water
2 cups (7 oz. jar) marshmallow cream
½ tsp vanilla
red food coloring

Directions

Combine sugar, egg white, and water in a double boiler. Beat with electric beater over boiling water until soft peaks form. Add marshmallow cream.Beat to stiff peaks. Remove from heat and beat in vanilla. Tint with food colouring.

Banana Bread

Ingredients

½ cup shortening
1 cup sugar
2 cups flour
½ tsp salt
3 eggs
1 tsp baking soda
3-4 mashed bananas
1 tsp vanilla
½ cup chopped nuts

Directions

Blend shortening and sugar. Beat in eggs one at a time. Add the dry ingredients. Add the bananas, nuts, and vanilla. Bake a 350° fahrenheit in a greased pan for 50-60 minutes.

Banana Fritters

Ingredients

2 ripe bananas (riper than normal, but not black), mashed
sugar
lemon juice
about 1 cup flour
pinch salt
1 egg
some milk

Directions

In a bowl, combine flour, salt, egg, and milk. Stir together; it should look like pancake batter.If too watery, add more flour; if too thick, add more milk. At right consistency, add mashed bananas and stir together. Heat oil in a pan and drop some batter in oil until brown. Flip over and brown the other side. Drain on paper towel and chill. When ready to eat, sprinkle with lemon juice and some sugar.

Best Ever Cheesecake

Ingredients

15 graham crackers
¼ cup butter
4 eggs, separated
1 cup sugar
1 tsp vanilla
1 pint sour cream
3 8 oz. packages of soft cream cheese

Directions

Set oven at 350° fahrenheit. Generously grease the bottom and sides of a 9 x 3 inch aluminum spring form pan. Roll graham crackers fine. Melt butter and combine it with 12 of the 15 rolled crachers. Cover the bottom of the pan with the mixture. Beat the egg whites stiff and set aside. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cream cheese, egg yolks, ¾ cup sugar, and vanilla. Beat until smooth. Fold in the stiff egg whites. Place the mixture in the pan and bake it for 40-50 minutes, until slightly brown. Remove it from the oven and pour sour cream mixed with remaining ¼ cup of sugar slowly over the top of the cake. Sprinkle the remainder of the graham cracker crumbs and return it to the oven at 475° fahrenheit for 5 minutes. Remove and cool. When cold, place in the refrigerator for at least 12 hours before serving.

Carrot Cake

Ingredients

1 cup sugar
⅔ cups vegetable oil
1 cup grated carrots
2 eggs
½ cup chopped nuts
1½ cup flour
1 tsp baking powder
1 tsp baking soda
1 tsp cinnamon
¼ tsp salt

Directions

Sift the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt together. Mix sugar and oil. Add dry ingredients. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Add carrots and nuts. Blend well. Bake for 55 minutes in a loaf pan at 375° fahrenheit for the first 20 minutes, then 350° fahrenheit for the rest of the time.

French Toast

Ingredients

1 12 oz. can of evaporated milk (pet milk)
2 eggs
vanilla
dash salt

Directions

Scramble the eggs in a big bowl, one that is big enough to dip the bread in. Add the can of evaporated milk, dash of salt (about ¼ teaspoon) and 2 teaspoons of vanilla (you can add more or less to your liking). Meanwhile, halve the bread (about 6 slices) which will give you 12 pieces (you might have enough batter to do more). Heat a skillet sprayed with pam or any kind of vegetable spray. Once heated, dip the bread halves in the batter a couple at a time, coating each side. Once coated, put the halves on the hot skillet, browining each side.

Funnel Cake

Ingredients

2½ cups all purpose flour
2½ tsp baking powder
1¼ tsp salt
¼ cup sugar
1½ cup milk
⅓ cup half and half
2 eggs, beaten lightly
vegetable oil
½ cup sifted powdered sugar
1 tsp ground cinnamon
⅛ tsp ground nutmeg

Directions

Combine flour, baking powder, and salt in medium mixing bowl; stir well. Ad ¼ cup sugar, stirring well to combine. Add milk, half and half, and eggs; beat at medium speed until smooth. Pour vegetable oil the depth of ¼ inch in large skillet and heat to 375° fahrenheit. Release batter with ⅜ inch opening funnel into hot oil in a slow, circular motion to form spiral. Fry each side for 1 minute or until golden brown. Drain on paper towl. Combine ½ cup powder sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg; stir well. Sprinkle over cakes. Serve warm. Makes about one dozen 5-inch cakes.

Kentucky Derby Pie

Ingredients

½ cup sugar
½ cup dark brown sugar
2 eggs
1 stick butter, melted
1 cup whole pecans
1 cup chocolate chips
2 tbs bourbon
1 unbaked pie shell

Directions

Combine sugars and egg. Beat until mixture is light beige in color. Combine melted butter, pecans, chocolate chips, and bourbon. Fold the two mixtures together. Pour into pie shell and bake at 375° fahrenheit for 40 minutes.

Marshmallow Cream Fudge

Ingredients

1 jar marshmallow cream
1½ cups sugar
⅔ cup evaporated milk
¼ cup butter or margarine
¼ tsp salt
2 cups (one 12 oz. package) semi-sweet chocolate morsels
½ cup chopped nuts
1 tsp vanilla extract

Directions

In medium pan combine marshmallow, sugar, evaporated milk, butter, and salt. Bring to full boil, stirring constanstly over moderate heat. Boil 5 minutes, stirring constantly. Remove from heat. Add morsels and stir until melted and mixture is smooth. Stir in nuts and vanilla. Pour into aluminum-foil-lined 8-inch square pan. Chill in refrigerator about 2 hours until firm.

Peanut Brittle

Ingredients

2 cups sugar
½ cup white corn syrup
¼ cup boiling water
2 cups raw peanuts
1 tsp baking soda

Directions

Put sugar, corn syrup, and water in large pot and let come to a boil. Add peanuts. Cook until they smell parched (about 20 minutes). Remove from heat. Add baking soda and stir. Pour onto well-greased pan. Break into pieces when cooled.

Peanut Butter Dream Bars

Ingredients

2 cups quick oats, uncooked
1½ cups flour
1 cup chopped peanuts
1 cup firmly packed brown sugar
1 tsp baking soda
¾ tsp salt
1 cup margarine, melted
1 can (14 oz.) sweetened condense milk
¾ cup peanut butter
1 cup plain m&m’s

Directions

Combine oats, flour, peanuts, sugar, soda, and salt. Mix well. Add margarine. Mix well until dry ingredients are thoroughly moistened and mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Reserve 1½ cups mixture. Press remaining mixture into bottom of greased 15½ x 10½ inch jelly roll pan. Bake at 375° fahrenheit for 12 minutes. Combine condensed milk and peanut butter in small bowl, mixing until well blended. Spread over partially baked crust to within ¼ inch from edge. Combine reserved crumb mixture and candies. Sprinkle evenly over condensed milk mixture, pressing lightly. Bake for 20-22 minutes until golden brown.

Peanut Clusters

Ingredients

1 bag (12 oz./ 2 cups) butterscotch morsels
1 bag 12 oz./ 2 cups) semi-sweet morsels
3 cups Spanish peanuts (with skin on)

Line cookie sheets with wax paper and set aside. Melt morsels together in pan on very low heat. Once melted, turn off heat and stir in peanuts. Drop by teaspoonful on wax-lined cookie sheets. Refrigerate to harden.

Pound Cake

Ingredients

½ lb. unsalted butter, softened
2½ cups sugar
7 large eggs at room temperature
3 cups cake flour, sifted twice
1 cup whipping cream
2 tsp pure vanilla extract

Directions

Note that eggs provide all the leavening for this dense, old-fashioned pound cake and that it must go into a cold oven.

Butter and flour a 12-cup Bundt pan. Thoroughly cream together butter and sugar. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in half of the flour, then the whipping cream, then the rest of the flour. Stir in vanilla. Pour batter into prepared pan. Important: set pan in cold oven; oven must not be preheated. Set temperature at 350° fahrenheit. Bake 60-70 minutes with rack a third of the way up from the bottom. Bake until cake tests done when a tester inserted near center comes out clean. Cool 10 minutes in pan. Remove from pan and cool thoroughly on wire cake rack, bottom side up.

Rice Krispies Treats

Ingredients

3 tbs margarine
1 package (10 oz. or 4 cups) miniature marshmallows
6 cups rice krispies

Directions

Spray 13 x 9 x 2 inch baking pan with cooking spray. In pan, melt margarine over low heat. Add the marshmallows and stir until completely melted. Remove from heat and stir in rice krispies. Press into greased baking pan and wait until cooled.

Snow Ball Cookies

Ingredients

½ cup (1 stick) butter or margarine
3 tbs confectioner's sugar
1 cup all-purpose flour
1 cup finely chopped pecans

Directions

Cream butter and 3 tablespoons of sugar until fluffy. Stir in flour and pecans; mix well. Refrigerate the dough for at least 2 hours. Shape into balls. Bake on ungreased baking sheet at 325° fahrenheit for 20 minutes. Remove from oven and let cool for just a little. While still warm, toss the cookies in a bag with powder sugar until coated. Let cool and enjoy!

Sticky Buns

Ingredients

½ cup melted butter
½ cup brown sugar
¼ cup sugar mixed with cinnamon
1 bag frozen rolls

Directions

Grease bundt pan. Put nuts in bottom of pan. Put rolls over nuts. Pour melted butter over rolls. Put brown sugar over butter. Put white sugar with cinnamon over brown sugar. Cover with foil and put pan on cookie sheet. Let sit covered in oven overnight. In the morning, remove foil and bake at 350° fahrenheit for 30-45 minutes.

Sugar Cookies

Ingredients

1 cup butter
1 cup sugar
2 eggs
1 tbs milk
1 tbs vanilla
3 cups flour, sifted
3 tsp baking powder

Directions

Cream butter and add sugar gradually. Add eggs, milk, and vanilla. Cream well. Add flour and baking powder sifted together. Cill 10 minutes. Roll and cut in desired shape. May be frosted or decorated with colored sugar or left just plain.

Vanishing Oatmeal Cookies

Ingredients

1 cup (2 sticks) margarine or butter, softened
1 cup firmly packed brown sugar
½ cup granulated sugar
2 eggs
1 tsp vanilla extract
1½ cup all purpose flour
1 tsp baking soda
1 tsp cinnamon
½ tsp salt (optional)
3 cups oats
1 cup raisins

Directions

Heat oven to 350°. Beat together margarine and sugars until creamy. Add eggs & vanilla; beat well. Add comvined flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt; mix well. Stir in oats and raisins; mix well. Drop by spoon on ungreased sheet. Bake 10-12 minutes.
